## Abstract

This paper records the strongest verified consequences of an inherited one-point spectral normal form for cylindrical singularities. The standing input, taken from earlier notes and not reproved here, is an exact corrected Jacobi field $z_p$, a canonical finite spectral jet $J_{p,\le m}$ below the forcing barrier, exact low-band annihilation of the residual $\zeta_{p,\le m}:=z_p-J_{p,\le m}$, and on the even branch a canonical cubic coefficient $\mathfrak j_3$.

The original program aimed at a nearby-point order-$3$ Whitney theorem. That theorem is not proved here. The main contribution of the present paper is instead mechanism-level and consists of four verified results.

First, the previously used one-input defect quantity collapses exactly to a synchronization term:
$$
P_{\le m}\Big(z_q-\mathcal T_{pq,r}z_p-(J_{q,\le m}-T_{a_{pq}}J_{p,\le m})\Big)
=
-\,P_{\le m}(\mathcal T_{pq,r}z_p-T_{a_{pq}}J_{p,\le m}).
$$
Consequently, that quantity does not by itself encode the $q$-jet and cannot imply Whitney compatibility.

Second, we prove a repaired defect-to-Whitney bridge: order-$m$ Whitney compatibility follows once one has both a true two-point low-band comparison for the corrected fields and a synchronization estimate against the translated Appell background.

Third, for a hypothetical pure cubic one-point correction we derive an exact low-band transport defect formula. It exhibits unavoidable lower-band terms generated by Appell translation.

Fourth, and most decisively, we prove a no-go theorem: for $0<\alpha<1$, there is no nontrivial smooth zero-order pointwise local correction jet
$$
(\mathfrak j_1,\mathfrak j_2)\longmapsto (c_1,c_2,c_3)
$$
that lifts generic order-$2$ Whitney data to order-$3$ compatibility. In particular, the missing order-$3$ theorem cannot be obtained from a pointwise algebraic correction of the lower jet alone.

The surviving open route is therefore genuinely two-point: either a nonlocal/covariant correction theorem, or a direct operator-level estimate for the common-gauge commutator.

## 1. Introduction

We work in the setting of cylindrical singularities, but the present paper does not attempt a full geometric regularity theorem. Instead it isolates what the inherited corrected spectral normal form really proves, and what it does not.

The standing one-point package supplies, for each singular point $p$, a corrected Jacobi field $z_p$, canonical center-band coefficients $\mathfrak j_r(p)$ below the forcing barrier, and an exact decomposition
$$
z_p=J_{p,\le m}+\zeta_{p,\le m},
\qquad
J_{p,\le m}(\tau)=\sum_{r=1}^m e^{-\lambda_r\tau}\mathbf T_r(\mathfrak j_r(p)),
$$
with
$$
P_{\le m}\zeta_{p,\le m}\equiv0.
$$
On the even branch one moreover has a canonical cubic coefficient $\mathfrak j_3$.

The original roadmap tried to turn this one-point information into a nearby-point order-$3$ Whitney theorem. The present paper shows that this cannot be done by the previously proposed one-input defect estimate, and that it cannot be repaired by any nontrivial zero-order pointwise local correction jet when $0<\alpha<1$.

The real contribution is thus narrower but mathematically sharper:

1. an exact collapse identity for the old combined defect quantity;
2. a repaired two-input defect-to-Whitney theorem;
3. an exact transport defect formula for a pure cubic correction ansatz;
4. a no-go theorem for all smooth zero-order local correction jets.

These results are proved completely. The larger order-$3$ nearby-point theorem remains open.

## 2. Standing input and notation

### 2.1. Cylindrical center bands

Let
$$
\mathcal C=\mathbf S^{n-k}(\sqrt{2(n-k)})\times L,
$$
where $L\cong\mathbf R^k$ is the axial factor and $E:=L^\perp$ is the normal factor inside $\mathbf R^{n+1}$. For each $r\ge 0$ let $\mathcal H_r^{OU}(L)$ be the degree-$r$ Ornstein--Uhlenbeck Hermite polynomials on $L$, and define the $r$-th center band by
$$
\mathcal J_r:=E\otimes \mathcal H_r^{OU}(L).
$$
Its Jacobi eigenvalue is
$$
\lambda_r=\frac{r-1}{2}.
$$
Let
$$
\mathbf T_r:E\otimes \operatorname{Sym}^r(L^*)\to \mathcal J_r
$$
be the tensor-to-Hermite identification.

Throughout, $P_{\le m}$ denotes projection to
$$
\mathcal J_{\le m}:=\bigoplus_{r=1}^m \mathcal J_r.
$$
We suppress parallel transport on the $p$-side tensors by working in a common gauge. The intrinsic form is obtained by reinserting $\mathcal P_{pq}$ where needed.

### 2.2. Standing one-point spectral package

The following is treated as input from earlier notes.

**Standing Input 2.1.** For each singular point $p$ and each integer $m$ below the forcing barrier there are canonical coefficients
$$
\mathfrak j_r(p)\in E_p\otimes \operatorname{Sym}^r(L_p^*),
\qquad 1\le r\le m,
$$
and an exact decomposition
$$
z_p(\tau)=J_{p,\le m}(\tau)+\zeta_{p,\le m}(\tau),
\qquad
J_{p,\le m}(\tau)=\sum_{r=1}^m e^{-\lambda_r\tau}\mathbf T_r(\mathfrak j_r(p)),
$$
such that
$$
P_{\le m}\zeta_{p,\le m}(\tau)\equiv 0.
$$
Moreover, for every bounded rescaled axial shift $a\in L_p$,
$$
\|P_{\le m}(T_a\zeta_{p,\le m}(\tau))\|_{L^2_\rho}
\le C(p,a)e^{-\lambda_{m+1}\tau}.
$$

On the even branch, the cubic coefficient $\mathfrak j_3(p)$ is canonical, so the previous statement applies with $m=3$ and $\lambda_4=\frac32$.

### 2.3. Appell translation

The Hermite basis is Appell on the axial factor. We will use the identity
$$
T_a\mathbf T_r(\eta)
=
\sum_{j=0}^r \binom{r}{j}
\mathbf T_j\big(\eta(a^{\otimes(r-j)},\cdot^{\otimes j})\big),
$$
proved in Appendix A.

At the scale
$$
\tau_r:=-2\log r,
$$
one has
$$
e^{-\lambda_j\tau_r}=r^{j-1}.
$$
This is the bridge between spectral order and spatial order.

## 3. The old one-input defect quantity and the repaired bridge

The inherited order-$m$ nearby-point defect was
$$
D^{\mathrm{old}}_{pq}(r):=
P_{\le m}
\Big(
 z_q(\tau_r)-\mathcal T_{pq,r}z_p(\tau_r)
 -\big(J_{q,\le m}(\tau_r)-T_{a_{pq}}J_{p,\le m}(\tau_r)\big)
\Big),
$$
where $r=|p-q|$, $\tau_r=-2\log r$, $a_{pq}=r^{-1}h_{pq}$, and $\mathcal T_{pq,r}$ is a common-gauge transport operator.

### 3.1. Exact collapse identity

**Proposition 3.1.** One has the exact identity
$$
D^{\mathrm{old}}_{pq}(r)
=
-\,P_{\le m}\big(\mathcal T_{pq,r}z_p(\tau_r)-T_{a_{pq}}J_{p,\le m}(\tau_r)\big).
$$

**Proof.** Since $P_{\le m}(z_q-J_{q,\le m})\equiv0$ by Standing Input 2.1,
$$
\begin{aligned}
D^{\mathrm{old}}_{pq}(r)
&=P_{\le m}\Big((z_q-J_{q,\le m})-(\mathcal T_{pq,r}z_p-T_{a_{pq}}J_{p,\le m})\Big) \\
&=-\,P_{\le m}\big(\mathcal T_{pq,r}z_p-T_{a_{pq}}J_{p,\le m}\big).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is exactly the claimed identity. $\square$

The identity shows that the old one-input quantity contains no $q$-jet information after low-band projection.

### 3.2. Why the old one-input bridge cannot imply Whitney compatibility

**Corollary 3.2.** The estimate
$$
\|D^{\mathrm{old}}_{pq}(r)\|_{L^2_\rho}\le Cr^{m-1+\alpha}
$$
by itself cannot imply order-$m$ Whitney compatibility of the jet.

**Proof.** By Proposition 3.1 the estimate only controls
$$
P_{\le m}(\mathcal T_{pq,r}z_p-T_{a_{pq}}J_{p,\le m}).
$$
It therefore does not see $J_{q,\le m}$ after low-band projection.

A concrete counterexample is immediate. Choose arbitrary tensors $\mathfrak j_r(p)$ and define
$$
z_p:=J_{p,\le m}.
$$
On the one-dimensional span of $z_p$, define the transport operator by
$$
\mathcal T_{pq,r}z_p:=T_{a_{pq}}J_{p,\le m}.
$$
Then
$$
D^{\mathrm{old}}_{pq}(r)\equiv0
$$
for every pair $(p,q)$, while the jets $\{\mathfrak j_r(q)\}$ are arbitrary and need not satisfy any Whitney relation. $\square$

The correct bridge must therefore use two separate estimates.

### 3.3. Repaired defect-to-Whitney bridge

**Theorem 3.3.** Fix $m\ge1$ and $0<\alpha\le1$. Suppose that for every sufficiently close pair $(p,q)$ one has both
$$
\|P_{\le m}(z_q(\tau_r)-\mathcal T_{pq,r}z_p(\tau_r))\|_{L^2_\rho}
\le Cr^{m-1+\alpha},
\tag{A}
$$
and
$$
\|P_{\le m}(\mathcal T_{pq,r}z_p(\tau_r)-T_{a_{pq}}J_{p,\le m}(\tau_r))\|_{L^2_\rho}
\le Cr^{m-1+\alpha}.
\tag{B}
$$
Then for each $1\le j\le m$,
$$
\mathfrak j_j(q)-
\sum_{\ell=j}^m \binom{\ell}{j}
\mathfrak j_\ell(p)(h_{pq}^{\otimes(\ell-j)},\cdot^{\otimes j})
=
O(r^{m-j+\alpha}).
$$
In intrinsic form, the $p$-side tensors are preceded by $\mathcal P_{pq}$.

**Proof.** By the triangle inequality and the two assumptions,
$$
\|P_{\le m}(z_q(\tau_r)-T_{a_{pq}}J_{p,\le m}(\tau_r))\|_{L^2_\rho}
\le Cr^{m-1+\alpha}.
\tag{3.1}
$$
Now project to the band $\mathcal J_j$. Since
$$
P_{\mathcal J_j}z_q(\tau_r)=r^{j-1}\mathbf T_j(\mathfrak j_j(q)),
$$
and the Appell law gives
$$
P_{\mathcal J_j}(T_{a_{pq}}J_{p,\le m}(\tau_r))
=
 r^{j-1}\mathbf T_j\left(
 \sum_{\ell=j}^m \binom{\ell}{j}
 \mathfrak j_\ell(p)(h_{pq}^{\otimes(\ell-j)},\cdot^{\otimes j})
 \right),
$$
we obtain
$$
r^{j-1}
\mathbf T_j\left(
\mathfrak j_j(q)-
\sum_{\ell=j}^m \binom{\ell}{j}
\mathfrak j_\ell(p)(h_{pq}^{\otimes(\ell-j)},\cdot^{\otimes j})
\right)
=
O(r^{m-1+\alpha}).
$$
Since $\mathbf T_j$ is an isomorphism on a finite-dimensional space, this is equivalent to
$$
\mathfrak j_j(q)-
\sum_{\ell=j}^m \binom{\ell}{j}
\mathfrak j_\ell(p)(h_{pq}^{\otimes(\ell-j)},\cdot^{\otimes j})
=
O(r^{m-j+\alpha}).
$$
This is exactly the order-$m$ Whitney compatibility relation. $\square$

### 3.4. Relation with the one-point package at cubic order

At order $m=3$, the identity of Proposition 3.1 can be rewritten using $z_p=J_{p,\le3}+\zeta_{p,\le3}$ as
$$
D^{\mathrm{old}}_{pq}(r)
=
-\,P_{\le3}(T_{a_{pq}}\zeta_{p,\le3}(\tau_r))
+P_{\le3}\big((T_{a_{pq}}-\mathcal T_{pq,r})z_p(\tau_r)\big).
$$
Standing Input 2.1 already gives
$$
\|P_{\le3}(T_{a_{pq}}\zeta_{p,\le3}(\tau_r))\|_{L^2_\rho}
\le Cr^3.
$$
Thus same-basepoint translation is harmless at cubic scale; the unresolved issue is genuinely two-point.

## 4. Exact transport defect of a pure cubic correction ansatz

The next question is whether the missing two-point estimate could come from a pointwise one-point correction depending only on the lower jet. We first analyze the pure cubic version.

Let
$$
w_p:=z_p-v_p.
$$
Assume the following strengthened pure cubic ansatz.

**Hypothesis 4.1.** There is a tensor field
$$
C_p\in E_p\otimes \operatorname{Sym}^3(L_p^*)
$$
and a remainder $\omega_p$ such that
$$
w_p(\tau)=e^{-\tau}\mathbf T_3(C_p)+\omega_p(\tau),
$$
and for every bounded rescaled axial shift $a$,
$$
\|P_{\le3}(T_a\omega_p(\tau))\|_{L^2_\rho}\le Ce^{-3\tau/2}.
$$

The remainder bound is essential: control of $P_{\le3}w_p$ at one basepoint is not enough, because high bands of $w_p$ can feed low bands after translation.

### 4.1. Exact defect formula

**Proposition 4.2.** Under Hypothesis 4.1, for every nearby pair $(p,q)$ one has
$$
\begin{aligned}
P_{\le3}\big(w_q(\tau_r)-T_{a_{pq}}w_p(\tau_r)\big)
&=
-3\,\mathbf T_1\big(C_p(h_{pq}^{\otimes2},\cdot)\big) \\
&\quad -3r\,\mathbf T_2\big(C_p(h_{pq},\cdot,\cdot)\big) \\
&\quad +r^2\mathbf T_3\big(C_q-C_p\big)
+O(r^3).
\end{aligned}
$$
Consequently,
$$
\begin{aligned}
P_{\le3}\big(w_q(\tau_r)-\mathcal T_{pq,r}w_p(\tau_r)\big)
&=
-3\,\mathbf T_1\big(C_p(h_{pq}^{\otimes2},\cdot)\big) \\
&\quad -3r\,\mathbf T_2\big(C_p(h_{pq},\cdot,\cdot)\big) \\
&\quad +r^2\mathbf T_3\big(C_q-C_p\big) \\
&\quad -P_{\le3}\big((\mathcal T_{pq,r}-T_{a_{pq}})w_p(\tau_r)\big)
+O(r^3).
\end{aligned}
$$

**Proof.** At $\tau_r=-2\log r$ one has $e^{-\tau_r}=r^2$. By the Appell law,
$$
T_{a_{pq}}\mathbf T_3(C_p)
=
\sum_{j=0}^3 \binom{3}{j}
\mathbf T_j\big(C_p(a_{pq}^{\otimes(3-j)},\cdot^{\otimes j})\big).
$$
After multiplying by $r^2$ and projecting to $\mathcal J_{\le3}$, the $j=0$ term disappears and we obtain
$$
P_{\le3}T_{a_{pq}}\big(e^{-\tau_r}\mathbf T_3(C_p)\big)
=
\sum_{j=1}^3 r^{j-1}\binom{3}{j}
\mathbf T_j\big(C_p(h_{pq}^{\otimes(3-j)},\cdot^{\otimes j})\big).
$$
Using Hypothesis 4.1 for the remainder at $a=a_{pq}$ gives an $O(r^3)$ error. Subtracting the corresponding $q$-side expression yields the first formula. The second follows by adding and subtracting $(\mathcal T_{pq,r}-T_{a_{pq}})w_p$. $\square$

The proposition exhibits the core obstruction: Appell translation of a pure cubic mode automatically generates lower bands of sizes $r^2$ and $r$ after rescaling.

### 4.2. A directional rigidity consequence

**Corollary 4.3.** Assume Hypothesis 4.1, and suppose in addition that
$$
\|P_{\le3}(w_q(\tau_r)-\mathcal T_{pq,r}w_p(\tau_r))\|_{L^2_\rho}\le Cr^{2+\alpha}
$$
and
$$
\|P_{\le3}((\mathcal T_{pq,r}-T_{a_{pq}})w_p(\tau_r))\|_{L^2_\rho}\le Cr^{2+\alpha}.
$$
Let $q_\nu\to p$ be a sequence such that
$$
\frac{h_{pq_\nu}}{|p-q_\nu|}\to \xi\in L_p.
$$
Then
$$
C_p(\xi,\cdot,\cdot)=0.
$$
In particular, if limiting directions fill the unit sphere in $L_p$, then $C_p=0$.

**Proof.** Project the second formula of Proposition 4.2 to $\mathcal J_2$:
$$
-3r\,\mathbf T_2\big(C_p(h_{pq},\cdot,\cdot)\big)=O(r^{2+\alpha}).
$$
Along the sequence $q_\nu\to p$, write $h_{pq_\nu}=r_\nu\xi+o(r_\nu)$. Then
$$
-3r_\nu^2\,\mathbf T_2\big(C_p(\xi,\cdot,\cdot)\big)+o(r_\nu^2)=O(r_\nu^{2+\alpha}).
$$
Divide by $r_\nu^2$ and let $\nu\to\infty$. Since $\mathbf T_2$ is injective,
$$
C_p(\xi,\cdot,\cdot)=0.
$$
If all directions occur, symmetry of $C_p$ implies $C_p=0$. $\square$

## 5. No-go theorem for zero-order local correction jets

We now rule out all smooth pointwise local correction jets in the sub-Lipschitz regime $0<\alpha<1$.

Set
$$
V_1:=E\otimes L^*,
\qquad
V_2:=E\otimes \operatorname{Sym}^2(L^*),
\qquad
V_3:=E\otimes \operatorname{Sym}^3(L^*).
$$
The intended interpretation is that $a\in V_1$ and $b\in V_2$ represent lower jet data $(\mathfrak j_1,\mathfrak j_2)$, while a correction jet would assign
$$
(c_1,c_2,c_3)=(F_1(a,b),F_2(a,b),F_3(a,b)).
$$

### 5.1. Model-path formulation

The next theorem is stated only for one-parameter model paths. This is enough, because any universal pointwise local lift on the full class of lower jets would have to satisfy these model tests.

**Theorem 5.1.** Let $0<\alpha<1$. Suppose
$$
F_1:V_1\times V_2\to V_1,
\qquad
F_2:V_1\times V_2\to V_2,
\qquad
F_3:V_1\times V_2\to V_3
$$
are smooth maps with the following property:

for every base point $(a_0,b_0)\in V_1\times V_2$, every direction $\xi\in L$, and every one-parameter family $(a_t,b_t)$ satisfying
$$
b_t-b_0=O(t^\alpha),
\tag{L2}
$$
$$
a_t-a_0-2t\,b_0(\xi,\cdot)=O(t^{1+\alpha}),
\tag{L1}
$$
as $t\downarrow0$, the induced corrections
$$
c_j(t):=F_j(a_t,b_t)
$$
satisfy
$$
c_3(t)-c_3(0)=O(t^\alpha),
\tag{C3}
$$
$$
c_2(t)-c_2(0)-3t\,c_3(0)(\xi,\cdot,\cdot)=O(t^{1+\alpha}),
\tag{C2}
$$
$$
c_1(t)-c_1(0)-2t\,c_2(0)(\xi,\cdot)-3t^2\,c_3(0)(\xi^{\otimes2},\cdot)=O(t^{2+\alpha}).
\tag{C1}
$$
Then
$$
F_2\equiv0,
\qquad
F_3\equiv0,
\qquad
F_1\text{ is constant}.
$$
If in addition $F_1(0,0)=0$, then $F_1\equiv0$.

The theorem says that no nontrivial smooth zero-order pointwise local correction jet can promote generic order-$2$ Whitney data to order-$3$ compatibility when $0<\alpha<1$.

### 5.2. First step: $F_1$ and $F_2$ cannot depend on $b$

**Proposition 5.2.** Under the hypotheses of Theorem 5.1,
$$
D_2F_1\equiv0,
\qquad
D_2F_2\equiv0.
$$
Therefore there exist smooth maps
$$
G_1:V_1\to V_1,
\qquad
G_2:V_1\to V_2
$$
such that
$$
F_1(a,b)=G_1(a),
\qquad
F_2(a,b)=G_2(a).
$$

**Proof.** Fix arbitrary $(a_0,b_0)$, $\xi\in L$, and $\beta\in V_2$. Consider the model path
$$
b_t:=b_0+t^\alpha\beta,
\qquad
a_t:=a_0+2t\,b_0(\xi,\cdot).
$$
Then (L2) and (L1) hold exactly.

Apply (C2):
$$
F_2(a_t,b_t)-F_2(a_0,b_0)-3t\,F_3(a_0,b_0)(\xi,\cdot,\cdot)=O(t^{1+\alpha}).
$$
By smoothness,
$$
F_2(a_t,b_t)-F_2(a_0,b_0)
=
D_1F_2(a_0,b_0)[2t\,b_0(\xi,\cdot)]
+
D_2F_2(a_0,b_0)[t^\alpha\beta]
+o(t^\alpha).
$$
Because $0<\alpha<1$, one has $t=o(t^\alpha)$. Divide by $t^\alpha$ and let $t\downarrow0$; the $t$-terms vanish and we obtain
$$
D_2F_2(a_0,b_0)[\beta]=0.
$$
Since $\beta$ and $(a_0,b_0)$ were arbitrary,
$$
D_2F_2\equiv0.
$$

The same path inserted into (C1) yields
$$
F_1(a_t,b_t)-F_1(a_0,b_0)-2t\,F_2(a_0,b_0)(\xi,\cdot)-3t^2F_3(a_0,b_0)(\xi^{\otimes2},\cdot)=O(t^{2+\alpha}).
$$
Again smoothness gives
$$
F_1(a_t,b_t)-F_1(a_0,b_0)
=
D_1F_1(a_0,b_0)[2t\,b_0(\xi,\cdot)]
+
D_2F_1(a_0,b_0)[t^\alpha\beta]
+o(t^\alpha).
$$
Divide by $t^\alpha$ and let $t\downarrow0$. Since $t=o(t^\alpha)$ and $t^2=o(t^\alpha)$, the only surviving term is the $D_2F_1$ piece, so
$$
D_2F_1(a_0,b_0)[\beta]=0.
$$
Hence $D_2F_1\equiv0$ as well. The claimed factorization follows. $\square$

### 5.3. Second step: the remaining correction jet is trivial

We need one linear-algebra fact.

**Lemma 5.3.** Fix nonzero $\xi\in L$ and any $\eta\in V_1=E\otimes L^*$. There exists $b\in V_2=E\otimes \operatorname{Sym}^2(L^*)$ such that
$$
b(\xi,\cdot)=\eta.
$$

**Proof.** Choose a linear functional $\lambda\in L^*$ with $\lambda(\xi)=1$. Define
$$
b(u,v):=\lambda(u)\eta(v)+\lambda(v)\eta(u)-\lambda(u)\lambda(v)\eta(\xi).
$$
This is symmetric in $(u,v)$ and satisfies
$$
b(\xi,v)=\eta(v)+\lambda(v)\eta(\xi)-\lambda(v)\eta(\xi)=\eta(v).
$$
So $b\in V_2$ has the required property. $\square$

**Proposition 5.4.** Under the hypotheses of Theorem 5.1,
$$
G_2\equiv0,
\qquad
F_3\equiv0,
\qquad
DG_1\equiv0.
$$
Consequently $F_1$ is constant.

**Proof.** Fix arbitrary $a_0\in V_1$, $b_0\in V_2$, and $\xi\in L$. Consider the constant-$b$ path
$$
b_t:=b_0,
\qquad
a_t:=a_0+2t\,b_0(\xi,\cdot).
$$
Then (L2) and (L1) again hold exactly.

Because $F_1(a,b)=G_1(a)$ and $F_2(a,b)=G_2(a)$ by Proposition 5.2, condition (C1) becomes
$$
G_1(a_t)-G_1(a_0)-2t\,G_2(a_0)(\xi,\cdot)-3t^2F_3(a_0,b_0)(\xi^{\otimes2},\cdot)=O(t^{2+\alpha}).
$$
Taylor expansion gives
$$
G_1(a_t)-G_1(a_0)=DG_1(a_0)[2t\,b_0(\xi,\cdot)]+O(t^2).
$$
Comparing the coefficients of $t$ yields
$$
DG_1(a_0)[2\,b_0(\xi,\cdot)]=2\,G_2(a_0)(\xi,\cdot).
\tag{5.1}
$$
Now fix $\xi$ and vary $b_0$. By Lemma 5.3, every $\eta\in V_1$ can be written as $\eta=b_0(\xi,\cdot)$ for a suitable $b_0\in V_2$. Hence (5.1) becomes
$$
DG_1(a_0)[2\eta]=2\,G_2(a_0)(\xi,\cdot)
\qquad\text{for every }\eta\in V_1.
$$
The right-hand side is independent of $\eta$, while the left-hand side is linear in $\eta$. Setting $\eta=0$ gives
$$
G_2(a_0)(\xi,\cdot)=0.
$$
Since $\xi$ was arbitrary,
$$
G_2(a_0)=0.
$$
As $a_0$ was arbitrary, $G_2\equiv0$.

Returning to (5.1), we now obtain
$$
DG_1(a_0)[\eta]=0
\qquad\text{for every }\eta\in V_1.
$$
Hence $DG_1(a_0)=0$. Since $a_0$ was arbitrary, $DG_1\equiv0$, so $G_1$ is constant.

Finally, insert the same constant-$b$ path into (C2). Since $G_2\equiv0$,
$$
-3t\,F_3(a_0,b_0)(\xi,\cdot,\cdot)=O(t^{1+\alpha}).
$$
Divide by $t$ and let $t\downarrow0$:
$$
F_3(a_0,b_0)(\xi,\cdot,\cdot)=0.
$$
Because $\xi$ was arbitrary, $F_3(a_0,b_0)=0$. Since $(a_0,b_0)$ was arbitrary, $F_3\equiv0$. $\square$

Combining Propositions 5.2 and 5.4 proves Theorem 5.1.

### 5.4. Consequence for the nearby-point program

**Corollary 5.5.** Let $0<\alpha<1$. There is no nontrivial smooth pointwise local lift
$$
(\mathfrak j_1,\mathfrak j_2)\mapsto (c_1,c_2,c_3)
$$
which, for generic order-$2$ Whitney data, produces order-$3$ compatibility purely by evaluation at the same point.

**Proof.** Any such lift would define smooth maps $F_j$ satisfying the hypotheses of Theorem 5.1 on all model paths. Hence it must be trivial. $\square$

This is the central rigidity statement of the paper.

## 6. What remains open

The paper does not prove the original nearby-point order-$3$ theorem. The standing one-point package already removes same-basepoint spectral pollution. By Section 3, the unresolved analytic content is genuinely two-point.

The live targets are therefore the following.

### 6.1. A true two-point corrected-field comparison

One needs an estimate of the form
$$
\|P_{\le3}(z_q(\tau_r)-\mathcal T_{pq,r}z_p(\tau_r))\|_{L^2_\rho}
\le Cr^{2+\alpha}.
$$
Together with the synchronization estimate
$$
\|P_{\le3}(\mathcal T_{pq,r}z_p(\tau_r)-T_{a_{pq}}J_{p,\le3}(\tau_r))\|_{L^2_\rho}
\le Cr^{2+\alpha},
$$
Theorem 3.3 would then give the cubic Whitney relations.

### 6.2. A nonlocal/covariant correction theorem or a direct commutator estimate

Because Corollary 5.5 rules out pointwise local correction jets, any successful proof must use at least one of the following:

1. a nonlocal or covariant correction theorem,
2. a direct operator estimate for
   $$
   P_{\le3}\big((\mathcal T_{pq,r}-T_{a_{pq}})z_p(\tau_r)\big),
   $$
3. extra geometric structure special to the actual singular-set class.

### 6.3. The first-order transport of $\mathfrak j_3$

Even top-band Hölder control is weaker than the full order-$3$ Taylor law. Formally, if one extends the Appell tower by one more band, then
$$
\mathfrak j_3^{(h)}
=
\mathfrak j_3+4\,\mathfrak j_4(h,\cdot^{\otimes3})+O(|h|^2).
$$
Thus the first-order transport of $\mathfrak j_3$ is governed by the next-band shadow. This is another reason the full block estimate, not merely continuity of $\mathfrak j_3$, is the correct target.

## Appendix A. Hermite--Appell translation identity

For completeness we record the identity used above. Let $H_r$ denote the Appell-normalized Ornstein--Uhlenbeck Hermite tensors on $L$. Their generating function is
$$
\exp\left(\langle y,\xi\rangle-\frac12|\xi|^2\right)
=
\sum_{r\ge0}\frac1{r!}H_r(y)[\xi^{\otimes r}].
$$
Translate $y\mapsto y+a$:
$$
\exp\left(\langle y+a,\xi\rangle-\frac12|\xi|^2\right)
=
\exp(\langle a,\xi\rangle)
\exp\left(\langle y,\xi\rangle-\frac12|\xi|^2\right).
$$
Expanding both sides and comparing the coefficient of $\xi^{\otimes r}$ gives
$$
H_r(y+a)
=
\sum_{j=0}^r \binom{r}{j}H_j(y)a^{\otimes(r-j)}.
$$
Tensoring with a normal vector yields
$$
T_a\mathbf T_r(\eta)
=
\sum_{j=0}^r \binom{r}{j}
\mathbf T_j\big(\eta(a^{\otimes(r-j)},\cdot^{\otimes j})\big).
$$

## Appendix B. Parity obstruction for a natural pure cubic correction

The no-go theorem of Section 5 is stronger than the following parity observation, but the latter is still useful as a quick structural test.

**Proposition B.1.** Let
$$
\mathbf C:V_1\times V_2\to V_3
$$
be smooth and $O(E)$-equivariant, meaning
$$
\mathbf C(Ra,Rb)=R\mathbf C(a,b)
\qquad\forall R\in O(E).
$$
If
$$
\mathbf C=\sum_{d\ge1}\mathbf C^{[d]}
$$
is the Taylor expansion into homogeneous terms of total degree $d$, then every even-degree term vanishes:
$$
\mathbf C^{[d]}=0
\qquad\text{for every even }d.
$$
In particular, there is no quadratic natural local correction term.

**Proof.** Apply equivariance with $R=-I_E$:
$$
\mathbf C(-a,-b)=-\mathbf C(a,b).
$$
Comparing homogeneous degree-$d$ pieces gives
$$
(-1)^d\mathbf C^{[d]}=-\mathbf C^{[d]}.
$$
If $d$ is even, this forces $\mathbf C^{[d]}=0$. $\square$
